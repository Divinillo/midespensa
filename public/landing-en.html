FAQ

Got questions?

How does the 7-day free trial work?
When you create your account for the first time, you automatically get 7 days of full Pro access — unlimited recipes, unlimited receipts, full nutrition scanner, PDF reports and everything else. No credit card needed. After 7 days, you can keep using the app for free (with some limits) or subscribe to Pro for $4.99/month.
What happens when the free trial ends?
You're automatically moved to the free plan: up to 5 recipes, 3 receipts, 5 AI suggestions and 5 scans in history. All your data is kept. If you want everything unlimited again, you can subscribe to Pro from the app settings.
Do I need to install anything?
No. MiDespensa works directly in your browser on any device. You can also install it as an app on your phone (PWA) without going through any app store: open midespensa.app in Chrome or Safari and tap "Add to Home Screen." It works just like a native app.
Does it work offline?
Yes. MiDespensa works offline — you can check your meal plan, shopping list and pantry even without a connection. You only need internet to sync data between devices or the first time you log in.
Which stores' receipts does it support?
MiDespensa can parse receipts from Walmart, Target, Kroger, Whole Foods, Costco, Trader Joe's and virtually any other US grocery store. Upload the PDF receipt you receive by email and it automatically detects products and prices.
Can I use the same account on multiple devices?
Yes. All your data syncs to the cloud. Access from your phone, tablet, and computer with the same account — changes appear on every device instantly.
What happens to my data if I cancel?
Your data stays in your account. If you cancel, you're moved back to the free plan — your meal plan, pantry and recipes are never deleted. You can reactivate your subscription at any time.
Is MiDespensa available on Android?
Yes! MiDespensa is available on Google Play and also as a PWA you can install directly from Chrome. Just visit midespensa.app in Chrome and tap the install icon in the address bar. It works exactly like a native app.
